Rachel Joy Scott

The girl whose hands touched millions of people's hearts

Rachel Joy Scott, the inspiration behind Rachel's Challenge, left behind a legacy of kindness.

Scott's life throughout the years:

  • 1981 - Born in Denver, Colorado.
  • 1989 - Moved to Littleton, Colorado.
  • 1993 - Committed herself to Christianity.
  • 1998 - Attended three churches: Celebration Christian Fellowship, Orchard Road Christian Center, and Trinity Christian Center. She choreographed Sunday service dances at Trinity and joined Breakthrough, a youth group at Orchard Road, where she became passionate about evangelism and discipleship. At this time, her closest friends also distanced themselves from her.
  • 1999 - Wrote essay, My Ethics; My Codes of Life, in which she stated the importance of acting in compassion. This later inspired her father to found Rachel's Challenge in her name.
  • 1999 - Died as a victim of the Columbine High School shooting.
  • 2001 - Rachel's Challenge, a nonprofit dedicated to advocating kindness and positivity while putting an end to school violence, bullying, and discrimination, is founded.
  • 2001 - Posthumously awarded National Kindness Award for Student of the Year by the Random Acts of Kindness Foundation.

"I have this theory that if one person will go out of their way to show compassion, then it will start a chain reaction of the same."

Rachel Joy Scott
